On this page

Evidence from the sources suggests that HelpmApp and similar digital, data-driven interventions are becoming critical components of modern homelessness strategies, particularly by addressing the information gap and re-engaging individuals through autonomy and rewards 1, 2.

1. Impact of Data-Driven Predictive Models ​

Case studies show that using data to identify and engage high-risk individuals before they become entrenched is highly effective 3, 4.

  • Los Angeles County: A predictive analytics model utilized hundreds of data points to identify individuals at high risk of homelessness 5, 6. Participants enrolled in this proactive prevention unit were 71% less likely to need street outreach or temporary shelters within 18 months 4, 5.
  • London Borough of Barking and Dagenham: This local model uses data on rent arrears and council tax to identify struggling residents 7, 8. By proactively contacting these individuals to offer support, they avoid the "crisis-led" model that currently dominates the system 7, 9.

2. The Role of Gamification and Behavioural Insights ​

HelpmApp’s use of a rewards system to incentivise engagement is supported by evidence regarding behavioural science and service design 10, 11.

  • Behavioural Science Trials: UK-based research showed that applying behavioural insights—such as simplifying appointment systems and using personalised language—increased service attendance rates from 50% to 67% 10.
  • Restoring Social Capital: Chronic homelessness erodes the pillars of reciprocity, recognition, and autonomy 12, 13. By rewarding users with "stars" for verified check-ins, HelpmApp allows unhoused individuals to act as autonomous social actors who can reciprocate support, thereby rebuilding the Psychological Capital (PsyCap)—hope and resilience—necessary for stability 14-16.

3. Digital Inclusion as a Re-engagement Tool ​

With over 60% of London's rough sleepers possessing smartphone access, digital platforms are now considered essential for reaching "hidden" populations 1, 2, 17.

  • The "No Wrong Door" Approach: London’s strategic shift towards "No Wrong Door" and "No First Night Out" principles relies on digital infrastructure to ensure people can access help simply and early 18, 19.
  • StreetLink and Digital Referrals: Digital tools like StreetLink have proven that the public can be empowered to connect rough sleepers with services quickly, reducing the time spent on the streets 20-22.

4. Systemic Efficiency and Cost-Offsets ​

Case studies of housing-led interventions like Housing First demonstrate that providing a stable foundation leads to dramatic reductions in emergency service usage 23, 24.

  • Manchester and Liverpool Pilots: After one year in a Housing First pilot, antisocial behaviour decreased from 29% to 12%, and contacts with the criminal justice system were halved 25.
  • House of Hope (London, Ontario): In its first year, this highly supportive housing model led to 83% fewer emergency department visits and 63% fewer days in criminal custody for its participants 26.
  • Cost Effectiveness: Preventing homelessness is estimated to be 2.5 to 14 times cheaper than providing 12 months of support after someone has already lost their home 27-29.

5. Re-engagement via "Navigators" ​

The use of specialized roles, similar to HelpmApp’s digital navigation function, has shown significant success in moving entrenched groups off the street 30, 31.

  • London Social Impact Bond (SIB): This programme used a "Navigator" model to provide a single point of contact for rough sleepers, helping them navigate a fragmented maze of 10,000+ service slots 32, 33. The evaluation found the model effective in supporting those with the most complex needs, achieving a 64.3% stable housing rate 34, 35.

**Systemic Analogy:**The current homelessness system is like a vast 10,000-page book where the pages have been torn out and scattered across a dark city. HelpmApp acts as both the digital binding and the flashlight, ensuring that no matter which page an unhoused person finds, they can instantly see the rest of the story and navigate safely to the final chapter of stability. 19, 36, 37
