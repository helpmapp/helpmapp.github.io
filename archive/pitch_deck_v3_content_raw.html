HelpmApp Pitch Deck v3.0 - Slide Content ​

Target Audience: Social Impact Funders, Grant Committees (e.g., UnLtd, BGV, LHF) Tone: Confident, data-driven, impact-focused. Key Message: HelpmApp is a live, de-risked platform that needs funding for market execution, not development.

Slide 1: Title Slide ​

Heading: HelpmApp: Connecting the Homeless with Services Through Gamification

Subheading: A live, production-ready platform for London. Seeking £70,000 for market execution.

Visual: HelpmApp logo and a screenshot of the live app homepage.

Slide 2: The Problem is Urgent and Solvable ​

Heading: 13,000+ Londoners Sleep Rough, Navigating a Maze of 10,000+ Fragmented Services

Content: People experiencing homelessness face a critical information gap. Service availability is opaque, hours change daily, and capacity fills quickly. This leads to wasted time, missed opportunities for aid, and a cycle of disengagement. Meanwhile, service providers and local authorities lack the data to understand utilization and coordinate effectively. The problem is not a lack of services; it is a lack of connection.

Key Data Points:

  • 13,231 people sleeping rough in London (latest count).
  • Over 10,000 service slots across hundreds of fragmented providers.
  • 60%+ of unhoused Londoners now have smartphone access.

Slide 3: HelpmApp is the Solution, and It's Already Live ​

Heading: A Single, User-Friendly Platform to Find, Access, and Engage with Critical Services

Content: HelpmApp is a mobile-first Progressive Web App (PWA) that aggregates verified support services onto a single, searchable map. Users can filter by category, location, and availability ("Open Now"). The platform is designed for low-bandwidth environments and is accessible on any smartphone. Unlike other solutions, HelpmApp is live and operational today at www.helpmapp.org.

Key Features:

  • Service Discovery: Interactive map and list view with advanced filtering.
  • Gamified Engagement: Users earn "stars" for check-ins, redeemable for real-world rewards.
  • Provider Portal: Service providers can manage their listings and access utilization data.

Slide 4: The Rewards System Drives Engagement and Generates Data ​

Heading: Gamification Incentivizes Service Utilization and Creates a Powerful Data Engine

Content: The core innovation of HelpmApp is its rewards system. Users earn "stars" by checking in at services. These stars can be redeemed for essential items like free coffee, bus tickets, hot meals, phone credit, and hygiene kits. This creates a positive feedback loop: users are incentivized to engage with services, and each check-in generates anonymized data that helps providers and policymakers understand service utilization in real-time.

Live Rewards Catalogue (5 Rewards Available):

  • Free Coffee (10 ⭐) - Sponsored by Local Cafes Association
  • Bus Ticket (15 ⭐) - Sponsored by Transport for London
  • Hot Meal (20 ⭐) - Sponsored by Community Kitchen
  • Phone Credit (25 ⭐) - Sponsored by Telecom Partners
  • Hygiene Kit (30 ⭐) - Sponsored by Healthcare Foundation

Slide 5: Live Traction Demonstrates Product-Market Fit ​

Heading: 270 Services Across All 32 London Boroughs – A 320% Increase from Initial Launch

Content: The platform has grown significantly since its initial launch. The current database includes 270 verified services across all 32 London boroughs, covering 9 critical categories: Food, Shelter, Healthcare, Legal Aid, Mental Health, Addiction Support, Clothing, Employment, and General Support. This comprehensive coverage validates the product's utility and readiness for user acquisition.

Slide 6: The "De-Risked" Investment Thesis ​

Heading: 100% of Your Investment Goes to Impact, Not Development

Content: Unlike most early-stage ventures, HelpmApp is not asking for funding to build a product. The product is built. Our £70,000 ask is focused entirely on market execution: funding the rewards pool, hiring peer advocates for outreach, and developing partnerships with service providers. This "de-risked" model means funders can be confident that their capital is deployed directly into community impact, not software development costs.

Slide 7: Zero-Cost Infrastructure Maximizes Capital Efficiency ​

Heading: A Modern, Serverless Stack Means Near-Zero Marginal Cost Per User

Content: HelpmApp is built on a "Zero-Cost Infrastructure" model using modern, serverless technologies. By leveraging the free tiers of Vercel (hosting), Supabase (database/auth), and Leaflet/OpenStreetMap (mapping), the platform has a near-zero marginal cost per user. This demonstrates exceptional capital efficiency and long-term sustainability, as the project does not require ongoing high-cost server maintenance.

Technology Stack:

  • Frontend: Next.js 15 (React)
  • Database: Supabase (PostgreSQL with PostGIS)
  • Hosting: Vercel
  • Mapping: Leaflet / OpenStreetMap

Slide 8: The Team and Governance Structure ​

Heading: A Community-Led CIC with Lived-Experience at the Core

Content: HelpmApp is incorporating as a UK Community Interest Company (CIC), a legal structure designed for social enterprises. This provides the transparency and accountability that grant-makers require, with an "Asset Lock" ensuring all funds are used for community benefit. The governance model includes an advisory board with representation from major homelessness charities, local authorities, technologists, and individuals with lived experience.

CIC Benefits:

  • Legal structure optimized for social impact grants.
  • "Asset Lock" ensures funds are used for community benefit.
  • Transparent reporting and governance.

Slide 9: The 12-Month Roadmap ​

Heading: From Live Platform to 2,000 Active Users and 3 Borough Pilots

Content: With funding secured, the 12-month roadmap focuses on aggressive user acquisition and partnership development. Phase 1 (Months 1-4) will target 500 active users in three pilot boroughs (Camden, Westminster, Lambeth) and onboard 50 service providers. Phase 2 (Months 5-12) will expand across all London boroughs, targeting 2,000 active users and securing 3 formal data-sharing pilots with local authorities.

Key Milestones:

  • Month 4: 500 active users, 50 service providers.
  • Month 12: 2,000 active users, 3 borough data-sharing pilots, 7+ corporate sponsors.

Heading: Join Us in Connecting London's Most Vulnerable to the Services They Need

Content: We are seeking £70,000 in grant funding to execute our market entry strategy. This investment will directly fund the rewards that incentivize engagement, the outreach that brings users onto the platform, and the partnerships that ensure long-term sustainability. The platform is live. The team is ready. We need your support to make a measurable impact on homelessness in London.
