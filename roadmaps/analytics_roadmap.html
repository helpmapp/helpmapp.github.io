HelpmApp Disruptive Analytics Roadmap: 2026 ​

Vision: Shift from "Service Directory" to "Homelessness Intelligence Platform".

Executive Summary ​

Current market incumbents (StreetLink, local council directories) focus on administrative data—lists of services and simple referral counts. HelpmApp will disrupt this by focusing on operational and outcome-based intelligence. We will provide real-time, predictive, and impact-driven insights that empower providers to optimize resources and funders to see tangible ROI.

Phase 1: Real-Time Operational Intelligence (Q2 2026) ​

Goal: Give providers "Air Traffic Control" visibility into their services.

1.1 Live Demand Heatmaps ​

Concept: Visualize real-time search and check-in activity across London.

  • The Disruptor: aggregated, anonymized "intent signals" (searches for "food in Hackney") vs. actual "utilization" (check-ins).
  • Value: Identifies demand/supply gaps as they happen. Alerts providers to surges in nearby need.

1.2 "Occupancy Now" Live Capacity ​

Concept: Real-time bed/service slot availability API.

  • The Disruptor: Move away from "call to check" to "book it now".
  • Implementation: Simple one-tap status updates for providers (Green/Amber/Red capacity) directly from their dashboard or via SMS bot.

1.3 Guest Demographics (Privacy-First) ​

Concept: Aggregated breakdown of who is using services (Age bands, Gender, Needs).

  • The Disruptor: Privacy-preserving "Zero-Knowledge" metrics. allow providers to tailor services (e.g., "We need more youth-specific support on Tuesdays") without compromising user anonymity.

Phase 2: Predictive Modeling & Forecasting (Q3 2026) ​

Goal: Stop reacting to homelessness; start predicting it.

2.1 Demand Casting ​

Concept: AI-driven prediction of service usage based on historical data + external factors.

  • Inputs: Weather forecasts (Met Office API), Public Holidays, Economic events.
  • The Disruptor: "Expect a 40% surge in shelter requests this weekend due to sub-zero forecast." content proactively sent to Admins.

2.2 Resource Optimization Engine ​

Concept: Suggest optimal opening hours and resource allocation.

  • Value: "Your food bank is busiest at 10 AM, but you open at 9 AM. shifting hours could serve 15% more people."

Phase 3: Impact & Outcomes Ecosystem (Q4 2026) ​

Goal: Prove the value of every pound spent.

3.1 Longitudinal Impact Tracking ​

Concept: Track user journeys across the ecosystem (anonymously).

  • The Disruptor: Move beyond "100 meals served" to "User X accessed food, then shelter, then employment support -> Exit from homelessness".
  • Metric: "Cycle Time" (Time from first check-in to 'dormant' status) as a proxy for successful intervention.

3.2 Social Value Calculator (SROI) ​

Concept: Auto-generated reports for grant applications.

  • Implementation: Map activity to standard Social Return on Investment values (e.g., HACT Social Value Bank).
  • Value: "This month, your service generated £45,000 in social value." – Absolute gold for funding bids.

3.3 The "Gap Map" ​

Concept: A geospatial tool for Commissioners and Councils.

  • Value: visualizes "Service Deserts"—areas with high search demand but low service density. Driving strategic commissioning of new services.

Technical Foundation (Prerequisites) ​

  1. Data Warehouse (Snowflake/BigQuery): To handle historical aggregation separate from the transactional app DB.
  2. Privacy Engine: Differential privacy algorithms to ensure no individual can ever be re-identified from aggregate maps.
  3. Open Data Standard (HSDS): Ensure all data can be exported in the Open Referral UK standard to force interoperability on the incumbents.

Why This Wins ​

Legacy platforms built by councils are designed to record what happened. HelpmApp's analytics are designed to change what happens. By giving providers military-grade intelligence, we make them more effective, which makes HelpmApp indispensable.
